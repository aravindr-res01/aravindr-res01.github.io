Implementing AI-Driven Decision Support in Agricultural Lending Through Predictive Analytics for Customer Relationship Management

Authors: Karthik Nallani Chakravartula, Aravind Raghu
Venue: Journal of Intelligent Management Decision · 5(1), 11–34
Year: 2026
DOI: 10.56578/jimd050102

Abstract

This work provides a complete methodology for adopting well-established AI methods (predictive analytics, LLM agents, forecasting) into Microsoft Dynamics 365 Customer Relationship Management (CRM) for agricultural lending. While not claiming that the algorithms are novel, this work contributes a pragmatic approach to implementing these algorithms that specifically address the regulatory, seasonal, and operational characteristics of agricultural finance, as regulated by the Farm Credit System.

It focuses on real-life constraints within the regulated financial services industry and measurable impacts that occurred. The paper provides a domain-oriented application of AI-CRM integration, with statistical testing including external validation on USDA datasets and benchmarking across peer Farm Credit institutions, as well as cross-institutional analysis.

Over an 18-month observation period, Farm Credit institutions reported statistically significant improvements, including average case resolution time falling by 28% (67.2h to 48.4h) and lead conversions improving by 35% (25.9% to 35.0%).

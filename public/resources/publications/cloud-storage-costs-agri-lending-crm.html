Reducing Cloud Storage Costs in Agri-Lending CRM Systems Using Intelligent Data Retention Policies

Authors: Karthik Nallani Chakravartula, Aravind Raghu

Type: Conference Paper

Venue: 2025 8th International Conference on Algorithms, Computing and Artificial Intelligence (ACAI)

Year: 2025

DOI: 10.1109/ACAI68217.2025.11406232

Abstract

Agri-lending Customer Relationship Management (CRM) systems store large volumes of highly sensitive data, including personal identification information, credit ratings, loan histories, ownership records, and creditworthiness indicators. While historical records are essential for informed lending, regulatory compliance, and risk mitigation, retaining full audit trails over long periods can significantly increase operational costs and degrade system performance.

This paper presents an intelligent data retention approach that applies a 30-day retention policy and archives historical audit records to Azure Blob Storage. To preserve accessibility and usability, the solution includes a custom API and an embedded React web application that allows lending officers to retrieve archived records seamlessly within the existing CRM workflow.

By using batch processing, paging, and throttling, the framework efficiently extracts and moves large volumes of audit trail data while improving performance. The approach reduces storage maintenance costs by up to 80%, improves CRM responsiveness, strengthens regulatory reliability, and enhances decision-making and productivity for loan officers. A case study using AgriBank validates the operational benefits of the proposed model.
